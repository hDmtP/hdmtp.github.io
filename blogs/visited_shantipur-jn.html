What's so special about Shantipur?
  • Noting much for most of the people in the world. Its personal preference.
  • Besides that Shantipur is famous for,
  • 1. Chaitanya Mahaprabhu's area and Ras Yatra. Born in present Nabadwip Dham (NDAE, I had also visited this station in Jan 2022) which is just in opposite across the Hoogly river from Shantipur. He used to dwell in those areas. So obviously many scriptures and temples regarding that are present there. Mahaprabhu Chaitanya is considered to be one of Lord Vishnu's Avatar.
  • 2. Various Suti Sarees are exported all around India from Shantipur.
  • 3. Invention of Rasgulla (debated). It was said to be first made in Phulia (FLU) which near Shantipur.
  • 4. Birth place of Krittibas Ojha. He was a medival Bengali poet (13-1400 AC) who translated The Epic Ramayana into Bengali. There is a staion made dedicated to his name Bathna Krittibas (BTKB). It is a halt station which most of the trains in that route skips except during Ras Yatra.

Why Shantipur is a Junction Station?
  • When people think of a junction station they generally think that a line comes and gets divided into two or more directions. Like Naihati JN where 4 tracks come from DDJ and 2 tracks goes towards BDC crossing the Hoogly river and 2 (+1 in progress) tracks goes towards KYI. It is like 'Y' shaped. --<
  • But for STB it is like upside down 'V' symbol. Like this, É…, except the vertex is not that sharo, it's curved.
  • Shantipur is providing an alternate route towards Krishnanagar City other than the SDAH main line via KNLP. So there are 2 lines for KNJ, RHA-KNLP-KNJ and RHA-KNLP-STB-KNJ. During the days of protest at Badkulla station in KNLP KNJ route Express trains towards Murshidabad/Lalgola (e.g. Bhagirati express, Dhano Dhanye express) used to go through the 2nd route.
  • But the STB-DTG-KNJ route is very very underrated. MPS is 25 kmph due to no level crossing in that 15 km route. MPS at KNLP-STB route is 80 kmph, bit slower than the SDAH main route (MPS: 100 kmph)
